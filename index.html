The Deep Transition Will Not Be a Green Super-Cycle

The risk field we are actually in.

The next thirty years will be shaped less by smooth “green growth” and more by social–biophysical whiplash across climate, food, energy, finance, and politics.

  • 3°C+ trajectories, hydrological and heat volatility, biodiversity collapse.
  • Oscillating supply chains and price spikes driving systemic whiplash.
  • Result: accelerating inequality, collapsing social contracts, rising war / security economies.

The real risk is not one crisis, but self-reinforcing loops that convert shocks into long-lasting whirlpools.

  • Biophysical shocks & volatility
  • Whiplash into everyday life
  • Acceleration of inequality
  • Fracture of the social contract
  • Othering, violence, closure
  • Self-perpetuating whirlpools of risk

Breaking the Whirlpool

Where and how civilization options intervene.

Civilization options are designed as “breakers” at the points where whirlpools form and deepen.

We target architectures that:

  • dampen volatility
  • buffer household whiplash
  • prevent inequality lock-in
  • preserve legitimacy
  • interrupt securitization defaults

If Breakers Fail: From Crisis to Self-Terminating Dynamics

What happens when whirlpools scale beyond control.

At sufficient scale and duration, these whirlpools become self-terminating: they destroy the institutional, ecological, and cognitive capacities needed for coordination at civilization scale.

  • Societies shift from negotiating shared futures to fighting over securitized, shrinking presents.
  • The outcome is not just “more crisis” but collapse of practical self-determination for large parts of humanity.
[08]

Optionality is the scarce resource of the 21st century

Why option space is the central object of concern.

In this risk field, the scarcest resource is option space—the real, structural ability of societies to still choose between multiple futures.

  • Whirlpools erase options by locking territories, classes, and communities into irreversible pathways.
  • They destroy the institutional and ecological slack required for experimentation, learning and course correction.
[09]

From Idea to Instrument: Measuring Option Space

Turning option space into something we can track and invest against.

Optionality becomes an investable object when we track the indicators that signal whether futures are opening or closing.

  • Option-space indicators: insurance retreat, heat mortality, grid deratings, food price volatility, migration pressure, rule-of-law indices, capital flight, disaster loss variance, etc.
  • Option-preserving returns: tail-risk reduction, mandate uptake, capital unlocked, legitimacy durability under stress.
[10]

From Retrospective Justice to Option-Preserving Justice

Justice as both repair and prevention of option collapse.

Justice now has two inseparable obligations: repairing historic harms and preventing futures where self-determination becomes structurally impossible.

  • Retrospective justice: recognize and repair extraction, dispossession, racialized violence.
  • Option-preserving justice: design transitions that are biophysically adequate, structurally just, and that keep or expand option space—especially for those historically denied it.
[11]

We have projects and policies. We lack strategic options.

Why existing capital deployment is structurally insufficient.

Most capital today funds projects and programmes; very little funds civilization-level options designed to shift power and coordination at the pace and scale now required. This leaves a structural gap: we are trying to navigate a 3°C world with institutions and coordination power built for a stable century.

  • Governance, economic, and institutional architectures remain largely unchanged since the 20th century.
  • There is no dedicated mechanism to build and anchor transnational or territorial ex-stitutions capable of holding cascading risk.

What Is a Civilization Option?

A precise definition of the unit of work.

A Civilization Option is a live, institutional alternative for managing a specific civilization-scale risk pattern—an ex-stitutional “operating-system fork” for a world under stress.

  • Not a project to fix a problem; a framework for organizing societies around that problem.
  • Includes: mandates, legal architectures, economic protocols, capitalization instruments, governance forms, data/tech infrastructures, and civic practices.

How We Specify an Option

Making options adoptable, comparable, and investable.

Each option follows a repeatable specification that makes it adoptable and investable.
